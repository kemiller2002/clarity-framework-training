Delivery modes • Fit • Decision sensitivity

Private teams or cohorts

Both modes teach the same discipline. The choice is about context: sensitivity, stakeholders, and how much shared reality you need inside the room.

The simplest rule

If the decision is confidential, politically loaded, or cross-functional: choose Private Teams. If the decision is primarily skill-building and shareable: choose Cohorts.

Both modes include

  • Live facilitation
  • Diagnosis of a real decision
  • Bounded sequence (reality → distortion → next step)
  • Artifacts: notes, diagnosis, constraints

No certification. No templates that replace judgment.

Compare the two options

Choose based on decision type and constraints, not preferences.

Private Team Session

  • Best for: sensitive decisions, internal alignment, high stakes
  • Participants: one team or leadership group
  • Outcome: shared diagnosis + next-step constraints for your decision
  • Confidentiality: highest (closed room)
  • Typical shape: 1 day or 2 half-days

Choose this when the real constraint is people, incentives, or cross-functional reality gaps.

Cohort Session

  • Best for: skill-building across leaders
  • Participants: small group from different orgs or teams
  • Outcome: practice reps + language for failure modes
  • Confidentiality: limited (shareable decisions only)
  • Typical shape: 1–2 days

Choose this when the primary goal is building diagnostic discipline you can carry back into your environment.

When each is appropriate

A few concrete decision patterns.

Choose Private Teams when…

  • There are multiple stakeholders with incompatible incentives
  • The decision is reputational or involves layoffs / reorgs
  • Information is confidential or not shareable
  • You need internal alignment more than general skill-building

Choose Cohorts when…

  • You want repeated practice reps
  • The decision can be discussed without exposure
  • You want cross-industry pattern recognition
  • Your goal is building a diagnostic habit

Neither is right when…

  • The decision is already made and you want validation
  • The conflict is moral/value-based and not resolvable by diagnosis
  • You want certainty or prediction guarantees
  • You want a tool to “win” internal debates

If you’re unsure, that’s normal. The intake is designed to identify which mode is appropriate—or whether we should decline.
