One sentence

Clarity makes decision failure visible—so judgment can operate in daylight.

A warning

  • The Clarity Framework is not a weapon.
  • It is not a debate tactic.
  • It is not “proof” you were right.

What Clarity does not do

These are non-negotiable. If you want these outcomes, you want something else.

  • Does not tell you what decision to make
  • Does not optimize outcomes
  • Does not eliminate risk
  • Does not replace expertise
  • Does not resolve value conflicts
  • Does not produce certainty on demand
  • Does not validate a pre-chosen solution

Why this matters

If a framework claims certainty, it invites overconfidence. If it claims optimization, it invites metric gaming. If it offers templates as answers, it trains dependency.

Clarity stays narrow so it stays honest: it surfaces distortion early, and it forces a clean separation between what is known and what is wished.
