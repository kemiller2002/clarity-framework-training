FAQ

These answers are deliberately constrained by the framework’s boundaries.

Is this a course?

No. It’s a facilitated working session. You bring a real decision. We practice diagnosis.

Do you provide templates or a playbook?

No. Templates are often used to bypass judgment. You’ll leave with artifacts from your real session, but not a universal script.

Is there certification?

No. Certification incentivizes performance theater. This work is about demonstrated judgment.

Who is this for?

People facing high-cost, hard-to-reverse decisions who want shared reality more than reassurance.

Common questions

Short answers, on purpose.

What do we actually do in the session?

We inventory reality, scan for distortion, and create next-step constraints that prevent premature commitment.

Can you do this for my team privately?

Yes. Private sessions are common when the decision is sensitive or involves cross-functional alignment.

Is this only for technical leaders?

No, but technical leaders tend to recognize the cost of premature commitment quickly. The framework is about decision failure in complex systems—technical or organizational.

Do we need a “real decision” to bring?

Ideally, yes. If you don’t have one, we can use a case pattern, but the training is most valuable when the stakes are real and the constraints are current.

Will this help us move faster?

Sometimes. The point isn’t speed. The point is avoiding expensive rework caused by committing inside a distorted frame. If it makes you slower at the beginning, that’s often the feature.

What if the real issue is political?

Then the framework will surface that quickly—and it may indicate the decision is not “diagnosable” in a technical sense. Clarity can’t resolve value conflicts or political incentives. It can make them visible.

What if someone uses Clarity to “win” an argument?

That’s misuse. The boundaries are designed to prevent justification behavior. If a team wants a debate weapon, we do not engage.
