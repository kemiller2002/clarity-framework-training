Why boundaries matter

Training model

The training is structured as facilitated practice. It is designed for decision-makers who need shared reality more than reassurance.

Format

  • 1–2 day engagements
  • Small cohort or private team
  • Live facilitation
  • Decision work, not slideware

What you practice

  • Diagnose distortion before debate
  • Separate uncertainty vs ambiguity
  • Surface hidden premises and false constraints
  • Decide what you cannot know yet (and what to do anyway)

What you leave with

  • A documented diagnosis of a real decision
  • A shared language for failure modes
  • A repeatable next-step discipline (not a playbook)

The goal is not comprehension. The goal is better decisions under constraint.

What a session looks like

A simple, bounded sequence that prevents premature solution selection.

1

Reality inventory

What is known, what is assumed, what is asserted, and what is missing.

2

Distortion scan

Where models replace reality: urgency, incentives, identity, and untested premises.

3

Next-step constraints

What must be true, what must be learned, and what decisions are reversible.
